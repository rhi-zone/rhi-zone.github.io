# Worldbuilding & Theme Exploration

Exploring cohesive naming through worldbuilding (post-NAMING.md clarity pivot).

## Why This Matters

**Naming is manipulation (the good kind).**

When people encounter your tools, the names create expectations:
- **Corporate names** (normalize, interconnect, DataPipeline) ‚Üí "this is serious production software, I need expertise, this scales to billions"
- **Cozy names** (bubbletea, lipgloss, uxn) ‚Üí "this is approachable, made by humans, I can try this casually"

The worldbuilding creates a **conceptual space**. If that space is cozy/collaborative/playful, people bring that energy. If it's corporate/technical/serious, they bring different energy.

**We want people to approach our tools with:**
- "This feels approachable, I can try it"
- "It's okay if I start small and imperfect"
- "This is made with care for humans"
- "This respects my time and doesn't demand expertise"

Not:
- "I need to read extensive docs first"
- "This is for serious production use only"
- "I should optimize and scale from day one"

**Our vision** (see docs/problems.md):
- Creation as play, not work
- First step trivially easy
- Permission to be imperfect
- Real connection (symmetric, not parasocial)
- Calm, respectful software
- Local-first, network-capable
- Collaboration should be natural (like games)
- Anti-corporate, anti-extraction, anti-metrics

If our names embody this vision, people *feel* it before reading any manifesto.

## Vision ‚Üí World Constraints

What kind of world embodies our values?

**Must feel:**
- Small-scale, human-sized (not infinite/overwhelming)
- Collaborative and symmetric (not hierarchical/broadcasting)
- Playful and creative (not serious/performative)

## Key Learnings from Exploration

### What We Discovered

**Namespace Reality:**
- Most obvious soft/gentle words are TAKEN on crates.io (moth, mist, bloom, glow, wisp, etc.)
- Soft woodland namespace is extremely crowded
- Compound words (gentlebreeze, softglow) are available but feel fantasy-location-coded ("Welcome to Brightthread Village")
- Need to be more creative than "pick cute words from nature"

**The Charmbracelet Insight:**
Charmbracelet doesn't just use "cute words" - they use **concrete nouns (THINGS)** that **metaphorically relate to their vision**:
- Vision: Making terminals beautiful/delightful
- Things: lipgloss (makes things pretty), bubbletea (delightful treat), glamour (makes things glamorous), glow (makes things shine)
- NOT random cute things, NOT adjectives/verbs, NOT abstract concepts

**Our Challenge:**
We need concrete nouns (things) that metaphorically relate to OUR vision:
- Vision: Unification simplifies fragmented complexity into something playful/approachable
- Things: ??? (what kinds of objects embody simplification/unification/making-big-things-playable?)

### What We're Stuck On

**The Core Question:**
What kinds of THINGS metaphorically capture "unification makes complexity simple enough to play with"?

Possible metaphor spaces explored:
- ‚ùì Distillation/essence (complex ‚Üí pure) - but sounds too scientific
- ‚ùì Toy-sized/miniatures (big ‚Üí small enough to hold) - but which specific toys?
- ‚ùì Optics/lenses (many views ‚Üí focused clarity) - most taken
- ‚ùì Craft/gentle creation - but which items aren't archaic (no thimbles/brooches)?

**Open Questions:**
- Do we even need categorical coherence, or just consistent feeling across different sources?
- Is there a metaphor space we haven't considered?
- Should some current names stay (dew, rescribe, moonlet work well)?
- Are we overthinking this?
