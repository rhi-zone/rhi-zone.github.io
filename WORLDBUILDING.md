# Worldbuilding & Theme Exploration

Exploring cohesive naming through worldbuilding (post-NAMING.md clarity pivot).

## Why This Matters

**Naming is manipulation (the good kind).**

When people encounter your tools, the names create expectations:
- **Corporate names** (normalize, interconnect, DataPipeline) ‚Üí "this is serious production software, I need expertise, this scales to billions"
- **Cozy names** (bubbletea, lipgloss, uxn) ‚Üí "this is approachable, made by humans, I can try this casually"

The worldbuilding creates a **conceptual space**. If that space is cozy/collaborative/playful, people bring that energy. If it's corporate/technical/serious, they bring different energy.

**We want people to approach our tools with:**
- "This feels approachable, I can try it"
- "It's okay if I start small and imperfect"
- "This is made with care for humans"
- "This respects my time and doesn't demand expertise"

Not:
- "I need to read extensive docs first"
- "This is for serious production use only"
- "I should optimize and scale from day one"

**Our vision** (see docs/problems.md):
- Creation as play, not work
- First step trivially easy
- Permission to be imperfect
- Real connection (symmetric, not parasocial)
- Calm, respectful software
- Local-first, network-capable
- Collaboration should be natural (like games)
- Anti-corporate, anti-extraction, anti-metrics

If our names embody this vision, people *feel* it before reading any manifesto.

## Vision ‚Üí World Constraints

What kind of world embodies our values?

**Must feel:**
- Small-scale, human-sized (not infinite/overwhelming)
- Collaborative and symmetric (not hierarchical/broadcasting)
- Playful and creative (not serious/performative)
- Calm and unhurried (not demanding/urgent)
- Welcoming of imperfection (not polished/curated)
- Made with care (not industrial/automated)

**Anti-patterns to avoid:**
- Corporate office vibes (productivity, efficiency, optimization)
- Industrial/factory (mass production, scale, pipelines)
- Laboratory (sterile, precise, scientific)
- Military/command (hierarchical, authoritative, control)

**Patterns that work:**
- Small creative communities (studios, workshops, gardens)
- Collaborative making (working together on shared artifacts)
- Exploration and discovery (not optimization)
- Tending and care (not extraction)

## Exploring the Strong Candidates

### üé® Workshop/Studio World

**The world:** Small creative studio where people make things collaboratively. Workstations, tools, materials. Sketches and experiments welcome. Indie game jam / art collective vibes.

**Mapping tools to this world:**
- Code intelligence ‚Üí **sketch**, **draft**, **outline**, **trace** (understanding structure)
- Procedural generation ‚Üí **kiln**, **forge**, **mold**, **cast** (forming/shaping)
- Document conversion ‚Üí **press**, **transcribe** (reproducing in different form)
- Runtime ‚Üí **bench**, **station**, **workspace** (where you actually work)
- Collaboration ‚Üí **studio**, **atelier** (shared creative space)
- Object store ‚Üí **archive**, **cabinet** (storing works)

**Vibe:** Playful making, permission to sketch/draft, collaborative creation

### üå± Garden World

**The world:** Digital garden/greenhouse where you tend creative projects. Growing, grafting, composting. Organic, messy, alive. Solarpunk/permacomputing.

**Mapping tools to this world:**
- Code intelligence ‚Üí **compost** (breaking down complexity into nutrients)
- Procedural generation ‚Üí **graft**, **propagate**, **hybridize** (combining/generating)
- Configuration ‚Üí **seedbed**, **plot**, **bed** (where things start)
- Collaboration ‚Üí **trellis**, **lattice** (supporting growth/connection)
- Object store ‚Üí **terrarium**, **plot**, **patch** (contained ecosystems)
- Runtime ‚Üí **greenhouse**, **nursery** (protected growing environment)

**Vibe:** Tending/care, organic growth, imperfection welcome

### üåø Hybrid: Creative Garden Workshop

**The world:** Greenhouse studio where you tend creative projects like plants. Making and growing blur together. You have tools (bench, kiln) AND living systems (plants, compost). Ghibli cottage energy.

**Why this works:**
- Combines playful making (studio) with organic tending (garden)
- "Growing" creative work feels less performative than "producing"
- Tools AND nature, making AND tending
- Permits both active creation and patient cultivation
- Rhizome (underground network) fits naturally in garden context

**Example aesthetic references:**
- Ghibli cottage workshops (Kiki's Delivery Service, Howl's Moving Castle)
- Stardew Valley's farm workshop hybrid
- Cozy game studios (Sokpop, Sokpop, Sokpop)
- 100 Rabbits aboard Pino (sailing = tending/navigating)
