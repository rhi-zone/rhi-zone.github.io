## Sensory Prose (existence) — The Core Thread

Session `1e13057d` is the dominant work of the day. Roughly **13 hours** of continuous, exploratory conversation building out a comprehensive design for prose generation that narrates sensations across all five (plus more) senses.

### What Happened

Started with a deceptively simple question: "what prose would narrate sensations?" Spiraled into a full design session covering:

- **Sensory dimensions**: sight, sound, touch, taste, smell, proprioception, interoception, pain/nociception, and less-obvious ones like humidity, kinesthetic sense
- **Contextual variability**: how the same physical stimulus reads completely differently based on neurodivergence, trauma, environment, identity (Deaf communities, sign language, language barriers, disability)
- **Trigger mechanisms**: realized the "three-category" distinction (independent/ambient/latent) was wrong—they're just types of triggers, and there's no fixed number
- **Content breadth**: from sensory fundamentals (hot surfaces, coil whine, tv static, nails on chalkboard) through cooking methods (Maillard reaction, burnt vs grilled), acquired tastes, texture sensitivity, empathetic responses to pain
- **Advanced dynamics**: fatigue/humidity interactions, stress modifiers on sensory gating, hygiene conditions and executive dysfunction, allergy acquisition, immune effects (MCAS, pregnancy as immunodeficient state)

### Design Artifacts Generated

Created multiple research documents with full literary examples:
- Linguistic toolkit for combining clauses
- Literary prose analysis of sensory sentences
- NLG aggregation and surface realization research

Generated 8+ concrete prose examples via subagents covering different states (calm/anxious/dissociated/overwhelmed indoor/outdoor), dialogue with regional variation, waking after poor sleep, cold flat mood walking.

### Architectural Pivot

After generating prose samples, analyzed: how do we *construct* this rather than hard-code it? Designed a probabilistic compositor system based on:
- **Observation sources** (what triggers prose generation)
- **Realization engine** (transforming observations into prose)
- **Lexical sets** (word/phrase banks organized by semantic role)
- **Sentence architectures** (participle phrases, adverbial fragments, declarative statements)

Key constraint: offline generation, no LLM calls during gameplay.

### Deepening the Model

Conversation evolved to dialogue (slang, vernacular, accent, ellipses, hesitation, cutting off), personality in speech ("to be fair," defensive phrases), emotional speech patterns (irritation, confrontation), and **narration tone itself** (choppiness/staccato vs flowing, momentum vs directional intentionality).

Recognized the prose style should reflect character state—delirium = muddy thoughts with blurred boundaries, exhaustion = different pacing/flow.

### Cultural & Literary Grounding

Added references: Porpentine (interactive fiction), ROMA (vocaloid with neurodivergence themes), Porter Robinson, Mitski. Identified a Google Drive of ROMA song sources for lyric analysis.

Recognized the gap: **most people aren't using LLMs for their generalization capabilities**—this project is trying to do something genuinely novel.

### Remaining Work

At session end, direction is clear but unfinished:

- Realization engine (transforming to text)
- Lexical set expansion
- Multi-observation architectures (not just single triggers)

The session includes self-aware notes that the design is still "super incomplete" but the shape is becoming clear enough to start building.
